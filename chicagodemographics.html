Life expectancy of Chicago Neighborhoods

A historical survey of life expectancy across Chicago neighborhoods over the last three decades, ordered by life expectancy in 1990, ascending. The Socratice Query Language pull and resulting table are displayed below.

Generally, the last three decades saw disparities across neighborhoods close, with some less affluent areas experiencing almost a 20 year increase in average life-expectancy. The cause is not immediately evident. It could be a result of better social programs or real economic growth in those areas. Conversely, it could be the result of gentrification. A more structured study would likely elucidate 

      
SELECT  _1990_life_expectancy, _2000_life_expectancy, 
_2010_life_expectancy, community_area 
  WHERE community_area NOT LIKE 'CHICAGO '
  ORDER BY _1990_life_expectancy

Source: Chicago public records: https://data.cityofchicago.org/Health-Human-Services/Public-Health-Statistics-Life-Expectancy-By-Commun/qjr3-bm53
